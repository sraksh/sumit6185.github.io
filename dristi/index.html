Mesra Chapter

X
Dristi, BIT Mesra Chapter, was founded when Vikas Pandey, a 4th year Mechanical Engineering student of the institute got together with like-minded individuals of his college and decided to do something about the problems plaguing the villages neighbouring the college, namely Panchauli and Masu. From its humble beginnings as ‘The BIT Mesra Boys’ Club’ back in the winters of 2013, Dristi today consists of upwards of 450 members, who are all dedicated to the purpose of betterment of the villagers, and helping them prosper and learn from educated students of the college. Team Dristi’s activities are overlooked by 3 major sub-groups: Diksha, Daamini, Daksha and Disha, which serve the purposes of enlightening the villagers about govt. schemes, encouraging women empowerment in the villages, imparting basic computer skills, and providing career counselling to the villages’ various students respectively. Also, F.C. Dristi and Dristi Tuition Classes help the children of these villages to understand the importance of sports and education in their life, and encourage them to do well in both spheres of their life. Dristi has been fundamental in the overall development of the aforementioned villages that we adopted, and Team Dristi, BIT Mesra will continue its journey, and therefore, the fulfillment of its purposes in the future too.

Deoghar Chapter

X
Dristi reached new heights with addition of Deoghar Chapter. Shubhanshu Shekhar Shukla (mechanical engineering) was deeply inspired by the concept and decided to bring it to BIT Deoghar. Today, the team which started with merely 2 guys stands tall with 72 proud members who have not only shown consistency, dedication and hard-work in their efforts but have also brought new and innovative ideas to the table which in turn has added new stars in concept of Dristi. The Team believes in a systematic and organized approach to solve problems and their projects i.e villages 'Simeria' and 'Ratanpur' have reaped many benefits of the same approach ever since the inception of this chapter in 2013. The Team conistently organises events of all 4 pillars at frequent intervals to ensure that no part of development cycle is left untoched.

About Dristi

BIT Mesra, since its foundation in 1955, is renowned for enriching and nurturing young minds in technical education but very less could be done for the villages encircling the campus. Though a number of individuals from the nearby villages have been employed here, the room for improvement always remains.And, this is how DRISTI began. The basic idea was to extend their parameters in terms of information, skills, education and recreation to guide them on the path of self sufficiency and success in future. The feasible targets were two villages-Masu and Panchauli which were in close vicinity of our college.

DISHA, as the name suggests, aims at providing a direction to young minds. It basically involves streamlining of calibres and potentials of students from middle and high school so that they can choose the best possible career for them. It also includes spreading awareness among the growing buds regarding various scholarships and vocational information trainings. Disha aims to bring such opportunities to the students so that they can be maximally benefitted for their future goal. Not solely devoted to youth, Disha also objectifies imparting improvised agricultural and cultivation related information to the farmers. Career counselling can not only transform future of rural youth but can also give a new outlook to the global career of our nation.

DIKSHA aims at creating general awareness regarding various government policies and schemes among the rural mass and the respective procedures to avail them, guiding them to relative information centres, helping them with the paperwork and online form-filling processes, and imparting information on the latest beneficial programmes launched by the government. Besides, effort is made in covering various aspects like banking facilities, scholarships, health schemes, provision of basic requirements like UID/ ration cards, along with awareness about various agricultural schemes. The surveys conducted and the regular interactions of the DRISTI members with the villagers help to keep an updated record of the statistics, which aid in scrutinising their various problem and hence work on them to improve their living standards. Health camps are conducted which focus on spreading awareness about health and hygiene, and prevention of common and seasonal diseases through check-ups and vaccinations. The DRISTI sports club also organises outdoor games like football for the village school students keeping their requirement for physical development in mind. DRISTI GREEN is another initiative which promotes environmental awareness by planting trees from time to time and instilling in everyone the urgency of the need for holding hands against the current scenario of the degrading environment, thus preventing the exploitation of natural resources.

DAMINI or Women Empowerment wing works on the task of upliftment of women in rural areas. Empowerment of women has taken an edge in the present era. This gender is excelling in every field and proving its metal in every arena that offers competition, whether it is social, political, and administrative or others. Damini makes rural women aware of issues pertaining to self-support, health and hygiene. Encouragement of women of common interests to form self-help groups, showcasing their creativity and talent, and most importantly, inculcating the need of self-dependence in them is what it aims at. It creates awareness among the villagers about the various health schemes and vaccinations pertaining especially to maternal and infant health care. It also aids them to counter addiction problems. Besides, it lays special emphasis on preparing socio-economically weak, gifted young minds of primary and middle school for the entrance exam of Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, a renowned chain of institutions all over our country. Damini aids women in their social, physical and psychological development, acting as the mirror reflecting their worth, making them realise their power as mothers of the future generations and helping them earn the reverence they deserve.

DAKSHA adds vibrant colours to the academic life of rural students by making it skillful in all ways possible. The wing imparts training of basic skills to the students in areas of spoken English, computer knowledge, creative arts and other conventional academic subjects. Students are taught the fundamental requisites of today’s work field. Computer knowledge and English fundamentals have become part and parcel of today’s world. The absence of such facilities in rural section makes the origin and need for Daksha become conspicuous for all. English is the most sought after mode of communication which in true sense conveys the flow of ideas and skills all through the globe, it will be unrealistic to imagine present era of livelihood without the use of English language. Computer knowledge is the first and foremost demand of current job trend, not a single job can be carried out without taps on the keyboard. To enhance the communication skills of the students, English speaking classes are organised for the village students and the curriculum is specifically designed to strengthen the foundation of the language. Computer classes are held in the institute campus on a weekly basis with the sole purpose of providing a technical exposure to the children. Interactive sessions are conducted in the village with a motive to demonstrate various scientific experiments to the students in a way different from the religious classroom teaching. The principles behind each are explained, developing interest in the little minds and also helping them in relating the daily life examples to the new concepts that they learn. To break the monotony, art and craft sessions are organised once in a while, throwing light on the creativity of the young talents in producing best out of recycled waste. It’s Daksha which aims for the transformation of layman into the man of skills.

Events

  • Computer Classes

    An effort to impart substantial level computer skills to young ones, so they don't remain unaware of latest technological development

  • English Speaking Classes

    Keeping the importance of English Language in mind, English classes are organized time to time to teach necessary english speaking skills

  • Magic of Science

    Young Scientific minds were unravelled when the event "Magic of Science" was organized to show how things of daily use can be used to perform some science magic

  • JNV preparation

    Classes for preparation of Jawahar Nehru Vidalaya enterance exams are organised on daily basis

  • Tuition Classes

    A highly active wing of DRISTI which aims at providing quality education in off school hours to the children of Panchaulli village.The tuition classes are held daily and include English classes as well. The English classes are held every week during the tuition class hours.

  • Art and Craft Camp

    Art and Craft camps are organized at frequent intervals to encourage the hidden artists of obscure places. It is a big creativity booster and has surfaced some real talented artists ever since its start.

  • Dristi Green

    An effort to grow more and more trees and make the surrounding clean and green.

  • Anti-Drug Addiction

    An Awareness campaign aimed at educating villagers about the ill effects of Drugs on their health and their social life and how can they be free of their addiction

  • General Awareness

    Another consistently organized campaign to increase social and general awareness among villagers

  • Girl Child Day

    With the motto "Har Kusum Ko Mile Vidya", Girl Child Day is celebrated every year. This is the platform to educated villagers about importance of educating their girl child and what all support they can seek from various sources for the same

  • Health Campaign

    Health and Sanitation Campaign are organized time to time to raise awareness about various health issues, diseases etc and how can they be prevented

  • Rain Water Harvesting

    An Event on Rain Water Harvesting was organised to teach various methods of recycling rain water. Villagers took active participation in this event and the event was largely successful

  • Women Empowerment

    Women Empowerment is one of the 4 pillars of Dristi. Regular discussion sessions are organized in villages to confront the problems faced by the women there and then ways are discussed to solve them at their personal level

  • Volunteer Development

    Because the volunteers are not just volunteers, they are a family. Many Events are organized for development and fun of volunteers. The Aim is to strengthen the bond of sharing and caring
